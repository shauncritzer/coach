My Story

I was Mr. Teen USA 1998. From the outside, everything looked perfect. But inside, I was falling apart.

What followed was 13 years of addiction that cost me everything - my career, my relationships, my sense of self.

But here's what I learned: addiction isn't a moral failing. It's a nervous system adaptation to overwhelming stress and unprocessed trauma.

I found recovery by treating the root cause - my dysregulated nervous system. Now, 13 years sober, I've dedicated my life to helping others find the same freedom.

The Problem

  • Process addictions (porn, gambling, shopping, food) hit the same dopamine pathways as substances
  • Willpower and "just stop" advice don't work - they ignore the nervous system dysregulation underneath
  • Traditional recovery often focuses on abstinence without addressing the trauma and nervous system state
  • You're stuck in cycles of shame, relapse, and feeling like you're fundamentally broken
